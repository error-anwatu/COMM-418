<h4> Pew Research Center: How Teens Use and View A I</h4>

<p>

Pew Research Center's new report, released February 24th,  on teenage usage of artifical intelligence and how their usage could be changing the rhetoric on the effects of artifical intelligence. The Center's reason for conducting this study is to get a better understanding of teenagers' experience in world where technology is rapidly developing, and gaining insight on parental concerns. The Center is attempting to bring those concepts together and get to the heart of the issue; digital literacy and emotional attachment to technology.

The Center's methodology includes details on their survery design and duration. The Center surveyed roughly 1,500 U.S. teenagers (13 to 17) and their parents online from September 25th to October 9th, 2025. 

The Center's results show that more than half of the respondents answered that they used artifical chatbots such as ChatGPT, Copilot, and Charater.ai as a substiute for a search engine (57%), to get assistance with homework (54%, or to entertain themselves (47%). However, only around 10% to 15% of respondents said that they used chatbots to have casual conversations or for emotional support and advice. 

Link to story: https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2026/02/24/how-teens-use-and-view-ai/
